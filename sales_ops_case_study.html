Sales Pipeline & Revenue Operations Analysis

Self-initiated case study · synthetic B2B SaaS sales pipeline (560 opportunities, 12 months)
The goal: act as the sales-ops analyst for a SaaS team, take a raw pipeline export, judge pipeline health, find where deals leak, forecast revenue, and turn it into recommendations a sales leader can act on. Built in Python/Excel-style analysis; charts below.

1 · Pipeline health

The biggest weighted value sits in Proposal and Negotiation, but a large block is still stuck early in Qualification. That early bulge is where forecast risk hides: value that looks like pipeline but rarely converts. Action: tighten qualification so early-stage value reflects real deals.

2 · Win rate by rep

Win rates range from 24% to 34% against a 29% team average. The spread is not random: the lowest rep is 10 points behind the top one. Action: have the top closer document their qualification and discovery approach as a playbook, and coach the bottom rep against it.

3 · Sales cycle: won vs lost

Won and lost deals close in almost the same time (46 vs 44 days). That matters: deals are not dying from slowness, so chasing "deal velocity" alone would not move win rate. The leak is qualification quality, not speed.

4 · Weighted revenue forecast

Applying stage-based close probabilities to the open pipeline gives a $2.32M weighted forecast, most of it carried by Negotiation and Proposal. This is the number a CFO can plan against, rather than the raw $4.97M open pipeline.

What I would tell the sales leader

  • Fix the source mix. Partner (36%) and Inbound (31%) convert far better than Outbound (22%) and Event (23%). Shift rep time and budget toward Partner and Inbound.
  • Coach the Central region. Central wins 20% vs West's 33%. A 13-point gap in one region is a process or skills problem worth a focused review.
  • Tighten qualification, not speed. Since won and lost cycles are equal, the win comes from better early-stage qualification, not faster closing. Add exit criteria before deals advance past Qualification.
  • Forecast on weighted pipeline. Report the $2.32M weighted number to leadership, not the $4.97M raw pipeline, so planning is grounded in real close probability.
